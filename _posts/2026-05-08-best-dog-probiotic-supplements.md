Purina FortiFlora lists *Enterococcus faecium* (1 billion CFU per sachet), which gave Bella consistent stool improvement, while VetriMega’s blend of *Lactobacillus* and *Bifidobacterium* (20 billion CFU per capsule) helped my senior poodle recover after antibiotics.  

**2. Match the delivery method to your dog’s preferences.**  
If your dog turns its nose up at powder, try Nutramax Proviable‑DC chews—the chicken flavor turned a reluctant Max into a treat‑eater. For dogs that already love wet meals, the NaturVet powder mixes in under a minute and hides the scent under a dab of broth.  

**3. Check added ingredients for synergy or irritation.**  
Zesty Paws adds prebiotic fibers that boosted Luna’s coat gloss, but the same fibers can cause extra gas in sensitive pups, as I observed with my first trial before switching to FortiFlora.  

**4. Consider cost versus potency.**  
While VetriScience offers a high‑CFU capsule, the price may be steep for daily use; FortiFlora provides a balance of potency and affordability for everyday maintenance.  

**5. Read recent owner reviews for patterns.**  
Consistent reports of reduced diarrhea and firmer stools across multiple platforms lined up with my own findings for FortiFlora and Proviable‑DC.  

Remember, probiotics complement a balanced diet, regular exercise, and veterinary care—they’re not a cure‑all.
