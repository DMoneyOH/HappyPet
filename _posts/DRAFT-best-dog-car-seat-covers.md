## Buying guide

Fit comes first. Measure your back seat before you buy, since a cover sized for a compact sedan will gap on a full-size SUV and bunch where you least want it. Look at the fabric weight next. A 600D or heavier fabric resists claw damage far better than lightweight polyester that frays after a few muddy trips. Waterproofing decides how the cover handles drool, rain, and the occasional accident, so check that the coating runs across the whole panel and not just the center strip.

Anchoring keeps the cover from creeping. Seat-anchor straps and headrest loops hold better than elastic corners alone, and nonslip backing stops the slow drift toward the footwell on every turn. Think about how often you carry human passengers too. A hammock protects more of the interior but blocks the seat for people, while a flat bench cover pulls out in seconds when a friend needs a ride. Cleaning matters for the long run, so a cover you can wipe down or toss in the wash beats one that traps odor after a wet day. Check the seatbelt openings as well, since a cover that seals them shut turns a quick buckle-up into a fight. The best dog car seat cover for you balances protection against how fast you need the seat back.
