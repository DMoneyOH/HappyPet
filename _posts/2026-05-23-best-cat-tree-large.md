## Buying Guide  

Choosing the **best large cat tree** involves more than just picking the tallest model. Start by measuring the space where you plan to place the tower; a wide base is essential for stability, especially with active jumpers. Look for solid materials—metal frames or reinforced wood—because cheap particle board can collapse under repeated use. Sisal‑wrapped posts are a must; they satisfy a cat’s natural urge to scratch and protect your furniture.  

Next, consider the number and type of resting spots. Padded condos, hammocks, and open platforms each serve a different purpose: enclosed condos for privacy, hammocks for a gentle sway, and open platforms for a panoramic view. If you have multiple cats, a tree with several independent perches reduces territorial disputes.  

Finally, think about maintenance. Removable, washable covers make cleaning easier, and replaceable sisal rope extends the tree’s lifespan. While budget is always a factor, remember that a well‑built cat tree can last years, saving you money on replacement furniture and scratching posts.
