### What We Like  

1. **Five Adjustable Speeds** – The low‑speed setting is perfect for puppies or dogs with thin nails, while the high‑speed mode tackles tough, overgrown claws in seconds. We appreciated the smooth transition between speeds; a single thumb button makes swapping effortless.  
2. **LED Guidance Light** – The tiny LED illuminates the nail tip, eliminating shadows and helping you avoid the quick. This feature alone saved us a few nervous moments that usually accompany blind filing.  
3. **Safety Guard Flexibility** – The guard's three positions (open, medium, closed) let you customize the distance between the bit and the nail. For dogs that are nervous about the vibration, the closed setting provides a reassuring barrier.  
4. **Quiet Operation** – Unlike rotary clippers that scream like a power tool, this grinder hums at a low, almost inaudible pitch. Bella barely flinched, and we could continue chatting without shouting over a whirring motor.  
5. **Long Battery Life & USB‑C Charging** – A single charge lasts for roughly 8–10 grooming sessions, according to the manufacturer's specs. The USB‑C port means you can top it up with the charger you already have for phones or tablets, which is a small but welcome convenience.  

### What Could Be Better  

1. **Bit Replacement Frequency** – The sanding bits wear down faster than we expected when used on larger breeds with thick nails. After a handful of sessions, the coarse bit started to lose its edge, requiring a replacement purchase sooner than we'd hoped.  
2. **Learning Curve for New Users** – The combination of speed settings, guard positions, and interchangeable bits can feel overwhelming at first. While the included manual is clear, we found it helpful to watch a short video tutorial before diving in. New dog owners might need a bit of patience before they feel confident.
