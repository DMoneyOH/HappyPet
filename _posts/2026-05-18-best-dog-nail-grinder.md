### What We Like

**1. LED Light That Actually Helps**
The highlighted LED shines directly onto the nail, making the pinkish quick visible even on dogs with dark nails. This single feature reduces the guesswork that causes most nicking accidents. It is not a gimmick -- it genuinely changes how confidently the tool can be used.

**2. Two Speeds, Used Correctly**
Low speed handles puppies, senior dogs, and anxious pets who need a gentler introduction. High speed moves through thick, overgrown nails on larger breeds without the tool bogging down. The two-speed design covers the full range without overcomplicating the experience.

**3. Three Grinding Ports**
The three port sizes accommodate nails across breeds and ages. A chihuahua and a German shepherd both get a snug fit rather than a loose, imprecise grind. Matching port to nail size also speeds up each session considerably.

**4. Diamond Drum Bit**
Diamond bits last significantly longer than sandpaper drums and cut more smoothly. The surface stays effective across many sessions without losing its edge, which matters for owners who groom weekly or bi-weekly.

**5. Quiet Motor**
Sound-sensitive dogs respond dramatically better to this grinder than to clippers or louder rotary tools. The motor hum sits low enough that most dogs habituate to it within a session or two.

**6. Three Hours of Cordless Runtime**
A full charge covers multiple dogs or multiple grooming sessions without needing to be plugged back in mid-use. USB recharging means no proprietary charger to track down.

### What Could Be Better

**1. Two Speeds Only**
Owners who groom multiple breeds with very different nail thicknesses may wish for a middle setting. The jump from low to high is workable but not perfectly smooth for every situation.

**2. White Housing Shows Wear**
The white finish scuffs and discolors with regular use faster than a darker housing would. Purely cosmetic, but worth noting for owners who prefer tools that look clean long-term.
